Tiger

Tigers are the largest wild cats in the world. Adults can weigh up to 363kg – that’s about the same as ten ten year olds! – and measure up to 3.3m!

  • Scientific Name: Panthera pardus
  • Average Length: 3.3 meters
  • Average Lifespan: 18 years
  • Habitat: tropical forests, evergreen forests, woodlands

The tiger is the largest species among the Felidae and classified in the genus Panthera. It is most recognizable for its dark vertical stripes on reddish-orange fur with a lighter underside. It is territorial and generally a solitary but social predator, requiring large contiguous areas of habitat, which support its requirements for prey and rearing of its offspring.
